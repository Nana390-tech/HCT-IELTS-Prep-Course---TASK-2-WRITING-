ORIENTATION

👋 Welcome to Task 2

In IELTS Academic Writing you write two pieces in 60 minutes. Task 1 describes a graph or chart. Task 2 — this app — is an essay where you give your opinion or discuss an idea. Task 2 is worth twice as many marks as Task 1, so it deserves the most care.

⏱️

40 minutes

Spend about 40 of your 60 minutes here. Plan for 5, write for 30, check for 5.

📝

250+ words

Write at least 250 words. Aim for 260–290. Below 250 loses marks; very long essays often lose quality.

⚖️

Worth 2×

Task 2 counts for two-thirds of your writing score. Always finish it.

🎯 How your essay is marked

Examiners give a band score for each of four areas, then average them. Each one is worth 25%.

25%Task Response
Do you answer every part of the question, take a clear position, and support your ideas?
25%Coherence & Cohesion
Is your essay logically organised into paragraphs, with smooth linking between ideas?
25%Lexical Resource
Do you use a good range of accurate vocabulary, including topic words and paraphrasing?
25%Grammatical Range & Accuracy
Do you use a variety of sentence structures with few errors?
Band 6 target: answer all parts of the task, organise clearly, and try a mix of simple and complex sentences — even with some mistakes, clear and complete writing scores well.

STEP 2 · UNDERSTAND

🔍 Analyse the question

Many students lose marks by answering only part of the question. Spend 1–2 minutes breaking the question into pieces before you plan.

✏️

1. Underline key words

Mark the most important nouns and the task words. Don't miss anything.

🎯

2. Find the topic

Say the topic in 1–2 words (e.g. "online learning", "city traffic").

🔒

3. Check restrictions

Is it about a specific group or place? (e.g. young children, developing countries).

📌

4. Identify the task

What exactly must you do? Often two things — answer both.

👀 Worked example

"In many cities, the use of private cars is causing serious air pollution. What problems does this cause, and what solutions can governments take?"
StepAnswer
TopicAir pollution from private cars
RestrictionIn cities; caused by private cars (not all transport)
Essay typeProblem & Solution
Task 1Explain the problems it causes
Task 2Suggest solutions (focus on what governments can do)
Watch out: if you wrote only about problems and forgot solutions — or wrote about all traffic instead of private cars — your Task Response score would drop. Always answer every part, within the limits given.
STEP 3 · PLAN

🏗️ The 4-paragraph blueprint

Almost every Task 2 essay uses the same simple shape. Fill it with the right content for your essay type.

¶1Introduction
Paraphrase the question + give your position / say what the essay will cover. (2–3 sentences)
¶2Body Paragraph 1
First main idea + reason + example + result. (4–5 sentences)
¶3Body Paragraph 2
Second main idea + reason + example + result. (4–5 sentences)
¶4Conclusion
Restate your position in new words + a final comment. (2 sentences)

🧩 Which content goes in each body paragraph?

The essay type decides what your two body paragraphs are about.

Essay typeBody Paragraph 1Body Paragraph 2
Opinion (agree/disagree)First reason for your viewSecond reason for your view
Discuss both viewsThe first viewThe second view (+ your opinion)
Advantages & disadvantagesAdvantagesDisadvantages
Problem & solutionThe problem(s)The solution(s)
Cause & solutionThe cause(s)The solution(s)
Cause & effectThe cause(s)The effect(s)
Golden rule: give roughly half the essay to each part of the task. If you write a lot about problems and only one line about solutions, the task is only "partly" answered.
STEP 4 · WRITE

✍️ Build a strong paragraph

A weak paragraph lists many ideas with no support. A strong one takes one idea and explains it fully. Use this 4-part chain:

IIdea — your main point (the topic sentence)
RReason — why is this true? (because…)
EExample — a specific case (For example…)
RResult — so what? the effect / consequence

See it in action

[Idea] One major benefit of working from home is that it saves a great deal of time. [Reason] This is because employees no longer have to spend hours commuting through heavy traffic to reach an office. [Example] For instance, a worker who once travelled two hours a day can now use that time to rest, exercise, or focus on tasks. [Result] As a result, many remote workers feel less stressed and are able to complete their work more efficiently.

🟦 Introduction frames

Two sentences are enough. Never copy the question — paraphrase it.

Sentence 1 (paraphrase): "It is often argued that ______. / Nowadays, ______ has become increasingly common."
Sentence 2 (your position / plan): "This essay will discuss both views before giving my opinion. / In my view, ______ because ______."

🟨 Conclusion frames

Transition + restate: "In conclusion / To sum up, ______ (your thesis in new words)."
Final comment: "It is therefore important that ______. / I believe this trend will ______ in the future."
Tip: introductions and conclusions earn few marks directly, so write them quickly and spend your time on strong body paragraphs.

Task 1 · Opinion essay (Agree / Disagree)

Some people believe that children should begin learning a foreign language as soon as they start primary school, rather than waiting until secondary school. To what extent do you agree or disagree?
0 words
Now compare. Read the model below and check: Did you state a clear opinion? Did you give a reason, an example, and a result in each paragraph?

It is sometimes argued that young children should study a foreign language from the very beginning of primary school instead of starting later. I strongly agree with this view, mainly because young learners acquire languages more naturally and because an early start builds lasting confidence.

The first reason I support an early start is that young children are especially good at picking up new languages. This is because at a young age children absorb sounds, vocabulary and grammar without consciously studying rules, much as they learn their first language. For example, a six-year-old who hears English songs and simple stories every day will often copy correct pronunciation almost effortlessly, whereas a teenager must memorise the same patterns through hard work. As a result, an early start allows learners to gain a natural accent that is very difficult to develop later.

A further benefit is that beginning early helps learners develop confidence that stays with them for years. When children use a foreign language from the start of their schooling, speaking it becomes a normal, everyday activity rather than a frightening test. For instance, pupils who present short talks in English from the age of seven tend to feel relaxed about speaking in public as teenagers. Consequently, they are far more likely to keep improving throughout their education and even into adulthood.

In conclusion, I firmly believe that foreign language teaching should begin in primary school. Because young children learn languages so easily and because an early start removes much of the fear of speaking, the advantages of this approach are simply too great to ignore.

Why it works: a clear opinion in the introduction, two fully-developed reasons (Idea → Reason → Example → Result), and a conclusion that restates the position in new words. ~290 words.

Task 2 · Discussion essay (Discuss both views + your opinion)

Some people think the internet has brought people closer together, while others believe it has made people more isolated. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.
0 words
Now compare. Check: Did you signal the first view as other people's opinion? Did you clearly give your own opinion?

The internet has changed the way people communicate, and opinions are divided about its social effects. While some claim it has strengthened human connections, others feel it has driven people apart. In my view, although there are real risks of isolation, the internet has mostly helped people stay closer than ever before.

On the one hand, those who believe the internet isolates people point to the decline of face-to-face contact. They argue that many individuals now spend hours alone scrolling through social media instead of meeting friends or family in person. A common example is a family sitting together at home while each member stares at a separate screen, barely speaking to one another. According to this view, online interaction is shallow and cannot replace the warmth of real relationships.

On the other hand, supporters of the internet emphasise how easily it allows people to maintain relationships across great distances. Thanks to video calls and messaging apps, families separated by work or migration can now see and speak to each other daily, which was almost impossible in the past. For instance, a worker living abroad can join a relative's birthday through a live video link and feel genuinely involved. In this way, technology keeps bonds alive that distance might otherwise have weakened.

In conclusion, while excessive screen time can certainly reduce face-to-face contact, I believe the internet ultimately brings people closer. Provided it is used in a balanced way, it offers powerful tools for staying connected with the people who matter most.

Why it works: view 1 is clearly signalled as other people's opinion ("those who believe…"), view 2 leads into the writer's own opinion, and the conclusion confirms the position. Both sides are balanced. ~280 words.

📐 Quick skeletons for the other types

Use these blueprints to practise the remaining essay types on your own.

Advantages & Disadvantages
¶1 Intro (say if benefits or drawbacks are greater)
¶2 Two advantages, explained
¶3 Two disadvantages, explained
¶4 Conclusion: which side wins, in new words
Problem & Solution
¶1 Intro paraphrasing the issue
¶2 1–2 problems, explained with effects
¶3 1–2 realistic solutions
¶4 Conclusion: summarise problem + best solution
Cause & Solution
¶1 Intro
¶2 Main causes of the situation
¶3 What governments / individuals can do
¶4 Conclusion
Cause & Effect
¶1 Intro
¶2 Main causes
¶3 Main effects on people / society
¶4 Conclusion
